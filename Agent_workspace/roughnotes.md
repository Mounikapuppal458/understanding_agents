# Rough Notes: Human Body and Its Architecture

- **Introduction**: The body is like an incredibly complex city.
- **Organization**: Smallest building blocks are cells -> tissues -> organs -> systems.
- **Systems**:
    - **Skeletal**: Skeleton provides structure, protection, and supports movement.
    - **Muscular**: Muscles attached to bones, allowing us to move.
    - **Nervous**: The brain and nerves are the command center (like the internet of the body).
    - **Circulatory**: Heart and blood vessels. Pumps nutrients and oxygen.
    - **Respiratory**: Lungs. Oxygen in, Carbon Dioxide out.
    - **Digestive**: Stomach and intestines. Food converted to fuel.
- **Integration**: The "teamwork" aspect. Example: Running requires muscles (movement), heart (pumping blood/oxygen), lungs (more air), and nervous system (coordination).
- **Maintenance**: Healthy diet, regular exercise, adequate sleep.
- **Conclusion**: A brief look at the wonder of biology.
